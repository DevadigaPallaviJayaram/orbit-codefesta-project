Module 3: Exoplanet Characteristics

Size and Mass

Gas Giants: These are massive exoplanets similar to Jupiter or Saturn in our solar system. They can be much larger, such as KELT-9b, a "hot Jupiter" with extreme temperatures. Neptunian Planets: These planets are similar in size to Neptune and Uranus. They are often classified as "ice giants" and are typically rich in water, ammonia, and methane. Super-Earths: These are exoplanets larger than Earth but smaller than Neptune, with masses between 2 to 10 Earth masses. Examples include Kepler-452b and K2-131b. Earth-like Planets: These planets are similar in size to Earth and may be rocky. They are of particular interest because they could potentially support life, depending on their distance from their star.

Orbital Characteristics

Orbital Distance: The distance between an exoplanet and its host star can vary greatly. Some planets orbit extremely close to their stars (with orbital periods of just a few days), while others orbit much farther away, taking years or even decades to complete one orbit. Eccentricity: Some exoplanets have highly elliptical orbits, meaning their distance from their star changes significantly over time. This can lead to extreme variations in climate. Orbital Resonances: Some exoplanets are found in resonant orbits with other planets in the same system, where their gravitational interactions create stable orbital patterns.

    Atmosphere

    Chemical Composition: Exoplanet atmospheres can vary widely. For instance, the atmosphere of WASP-121b contains glowing water vapor and metals due to its proximity to its star. Others, like HD 189733b, have atmospheres rich in methane, ammonia, and other gases. Temperature: The temperature of an exoplanet's atmosphere depends on its proximity to its host star. Some exoplanets, like KELT-9b, experience temperatures hotter than many stars, while others, like rogue planets, drift in space without a star and are extremely cold. Clouds and Hazes: Many exoplanets have clouds made of exotic materials, such as silicates or metal oxides, and can have hazes of complex hydrocarbons.

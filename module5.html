Module 5: Goals and Future of NASA's Exoplanet Exploration Program

NASA's Exoplanet Exploration Program is focused on discovering and studying planets beyond our solar system, with a vision that encompasses a variety of goals and future initiatives. Here’s an overview:

Goals of the Exoplanet Exploration Program

Discover Exoplanets: The primary goal is to identify and catalog as many exoplanets as possible, particularly those that are similar in size and temperature to Earth.

Characterize Atmospheres:

Studying the atmospheres of exoplanets to understand their composition, weather patterns, and potential habitability. This includes detecting gases like water vapor, carbon dioxide, and methane.

Identify Potentially Habitable Worlds:

Focusing on planets located in the habitable zone of their stars—where conditions might be right for liquid water and, by extension, life.

Understand Planetary Systems:

Investigating the formation and evolution of planetary systems to learn about the diversity of planetary architectures and conditions that lead to planet formation.

Search for Biosignatures:

Developing methods to detect potential signs of life (biosignatures) in the atmospheres of exoplanets.
